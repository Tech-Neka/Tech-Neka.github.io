RESEARCH PROJECT DESCRIPTION

INTRODUCTION

For the last 30 years, various social scientists have been 'gallupping' the entire globe in search of the true meaning, the value, the propellants and the deterrents of happiness. Their findings have been published by various media outlets or organisations at various times, the first and most consistent of them being The Sustainable Develop Solutions Network which publishes it yearly in a most comprehensive report called The World Happiness Report.

This report is largely based on survey data from the Gallup World Poll which interviews about 150 countries yearly on their views about happiness. It uses a cantril ladder on a scale of 1 to 10 to evaluate happiness based on individuals thoughts and views of their happiness feelings each day.

Among their findings, the researchers singled out six main factors that have been shown to influence happiness. They include GDP per capita, ideal social networks groups, political freedom, generosity and good governance. However, as can be noticed in this research from the time-series data obtained, actual measures used to estimate happiness rankings differ from country to country and year to year.

PURPOSE

The purpose of this project is to investigate the latent or hidden factors influencing happiness by using Statistical tools like Regression and forecasting.

The Research questions will be formed by identifying and selecting relevant questions from those asked by inquisitive citizens or private researchers, with the aim of creating a happier society for all.


RESEARCH QUESTIONS

  1. What is happiness?
  2. What factors bring about true happiness?
  3. What percentage of people are truly happy?
  4. Does average weather influence happiness?
  5. Is there a correlation between region lived in and happiness?
  6. Does coastal geagraphy account for higher happiness scores?
  7. What countries will consist the top ten for the next five years?
  8. Do country colours and symbols have any relaionship to happiness?
  9. What counntries will make a significant climb in rank for the next five years?


TESTS OF SIGNIFICANCE


There are three hypotheses this project shall consider, all one-tailed, at 95% confidence levels and based on the research questions.

HYPOTHESES

  1. Weather
    Ho: Weather does not influence moods
    H1: Colder climates impact greater levels of happiness in citizens

  2. Location
    Ho: Latitude does not influence happiness
    H1: The further a country is from the equator, the happier it's people tend to be

  3. Coastal Geography
    Ho: The presence of large water bodies has no impact on people's feelings
    H1: Citizens of countries with large coastal lines or high percentages of surrounding water are happier than others.

DEGREE OF CONFIDENCE

Alpha is 0.05

DECISION CRITERIA

For all computations, reject the null hypothesis where the calculated probability is less than the critical value.
