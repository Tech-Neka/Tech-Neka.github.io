USER STORIES AND STATISTICS

USER STORIES

User stories are basically tools used by agile software developers to get directions about the product they are creating from the customers. They are an opportunity for users to fully describe in detail their exact product specifications based on its functionality and benefit. This, together with the list or sequence of next steps to be taken by the product owner and his team, make up the story features. They are best represented in tables.

Data Analytics presents a slightly different view and variation. In a social research such as this one, the purpose or objective is basically a review of the topic at hand, wherein the customer, being in this case a concerned and curious citizen, requires an offering, and not a physical product. It features the research questions, and the answers and optimization strategies obtained and proferred by the analyst as a result of the research. Finally it presents the results as charts and dashboards for attractive visualization.


TABLE: WORLD HAPPINESS RESEARCH USER STORIES

KEY QUESTION ANSWER/INSIGHT ACTION/OPTIMIZING STRATEGIES
What is happiness The absence of worries and the presence of contentment Focus on resolving or Removing causes of worry
What factors bring about true happiness High or sufficient income, good governance, healthy social integration and time value among others Work towards cultivating these factors or habits
What percentage of people are truly happy About 30% of surveyed countries have happiness scores of over 60%; Citizens from less happy countries should learn and do more to upgrade their daily lifestyles and ultimately their happiness
Does average weather influence happiness There is an inverse correlation of about 42% between them. The colder the region, the happier it citizens. Hotter climates should invest in cooling machinery like air conditions to reduce ecessive heat
Is there a correlation between region lived in and happiness A correlation of 30% exists between the variables Take a holiday in such places to discover other secrets to happiness
What countries will top the list for the next five years Norway, Denmark, Finland, Switzerland, Netherlands, Iceland, New Zealand, United Kingdom, Canada, Malta and Sweden Other countries should learn best practices from them to improve overall happiness
Do colours or symbolism have anything to do with happiness The flags of the happiest countries have mostly cross symbols on them and are coloured in a combination including red, blue and white Perhaps other countries could brighten their flags
What countries will make a significant climb in rank in the next five years? United Kingdom Malta, Bahrain, Kosovo and Jamaica This is laudable and commendable. The citizens should keep up the good work and healthy habits
